Order-to-Cash (O2C) Process

The Order-to-Cash (O2C) process is a critical business cycle that involves the steps from order intake to final payment. Here's a simplified overview of the process, incorporating the specific details of your scenario:

  1. Order Intake and Processing:
    • Import Sales Orders: Sales orders are imported from various web shops into the system.
    • Order Validation: The system verifies the order details, such as product availability, pricing, and shipping address.
    • Order Confirmation: Once validated, the order is confirmed and a confirmation is sent to the customer.
  2. Order Fulfillment:
    • Commissioning: This can happen either within the system or through third-party logistics providers (3PLs). It involves picking, packing, and preparing the products for shipment.
    • Shipping: The packaged products are shipped to the customer's address.
    • Shipping Notification: A shipping notification is sent to the customer, including tracking information.
  3. Invoicing:
    • Invoice Generation: Once the order is shipped, an invoice is generated and sent to the customer.
    • Invoice Delivery: The invoice can be delivered electronically or physically.
  4. Payment Processing:
    • Payment Receipt: The system records payments received from customers, whether by credit card, bank transfer, or
      • other methods.
    • Payment Reconciliation: Payments are reconciled with invoices to ensure accuracy.
  5. Cash Application:
    • Cash Allocation: Payments are allocated to specific invoices.
    • Cash Posting: The allocated payments are posted to the accounting system.
  6. Credit and Collections:
    • Credit Checks: Creditworthiness of customers may be assessed, especially for new customers or large orders.
    • Credit Limits: Credit limits may be set for customers.
    • Collections: If payments are delayed, collection efforts may be initiated.
